Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 15, 2026 · Effective date: April 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Extra Space Asia group of companies, established in Singapore with its registered office at 301 Boon Keng Road, Singapore 339779 ("ESA", "we", "us", "our"), collects, uses, discloses, stores, and protects personal data in connection with the ESA Backend Portal at backend.extraspace.com.sg (the "Portal").

The Portal is a strictly internal business application. Access is limited to authorized ESA employees and contractors operating our self-storage business across Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Japan, and Korea. The Portal is not offered to the general public, has no self-service sign-up, is not marketed externally, and contains no consumer-facing features. All accounts are provisioned by ESA administrators; unauthorized users cannot access any authenticated feature. Consumer-facing services and their privacy notice live on our public website at extraspaceasia.com/sg. This Policy governs the Portal only.

We handle personal data in accordance with the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act 2012 ("PDPA") and other applicable laws, and consistent with the ESA group's overall Corporate Privacy Policy. Where anything in this Portal Policy conflicts with the Corporate Privacy Policy in respect of Portal processing, this Portal Policy prevails because it reflects how data is actually handled inside the Portal environment.

1. Scope of this Policy

This Policy covers:

  • Personal data of ESA staff and authorized contractors who sign in to the Portal.
  • Personal data of ESA customers that is processed through the Portal in the course of operating our storage facilities (for example, reservation and billing records).
  • Data obtained from third-party services such as Google, Microsoft, and our property management system when those services are accessed through the Portal.

This Policy does not cover ESA's public websites, customer mobile applications, or in-store customer interactions, which are governed by separate privacy notices.

2. Personal Data We Collect

2.1 Information you provide directly

  • Account profile: full name, work email address, employee identifier, job title, department, assigned site(s), and role.
  • Contact details used for notifications and audit correspondence.
  • Any content you submit through the Portal, such as internal notes, uploaded documents, or tool inputs.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Authentication events: sign-in timestamps, sign-in method (password, Microsoft SSO, Google SSO), and multi-factor status.
  • Session metadata: session identifiers, device and browser type, operating system, and approximate location derived from IP address.
  • Activity logs: pages viewed, actions taken, API endpoints called, records created or modified, and the time of each action. These logs are retained for security, fraud prevention, audit, and regulatory purposes.
  • Diagnostic data: error reports, request identifiers, and performance metrics generated by the application and its infrastructure.

2.3 Information received from third parties

  • Identity providers. When you sign in using Microsoft or Google, we receive your name, email, a unique subject identifier, and (for Google) any additional OAuth scopes you grant.
  • Google APIs. Where authorized, the Portal retrieves advertising data from Google Ads and related services so staff can review campaign performance.
  • Internal systems. The Portal integrates with ESA's property management system (StorageMaker / SMD CallCenterWs), SugarCRM, EmbedSocial, Azure services, and Google BigQuery to consolidate operational data.

2.4 Customer personal data processed through the Portal

In the ordinary course of business, the Portal processes personal data about ESA customers — including names, contact details, storage unit assignments, reservation details, billing records, and correspondence — so that authorized staff can service those customers. This customer data is collected under separate customer-facing privacy notices and is handled by the Portal only on ESA's behalf as data controller.

3. How We Use Personal Data

We use personal data for the following purposes:

  • Authentication and access control — to verify your identity, establish a session, and enforce role-based permissions.
  • Business operations — to manage reservations, billing, discount plans, unit inventory, smart-lock assignments, ECRI processes, customer service workflows, and reporting.
  • Analytics and reporting — to produce occupancy, revenue, and operational dashboards sourced from our data warehouse.
  • Security and fraud prevention — to detect unauthorized access, investigate suspicious activity, enforce rate limits, and maintain audit trails.
  • Compliance and legal obligations — to meet tax, accounting, consumer protection, anti-money-laundering, and data protection requirements.
  • Service improvement — to monitor performance, debug errors, and improve Portal reliability and usability.
  • Internal communications — to send operational notifications, alerts, and system messages to staff.

Our legal bases for processing under the PDPA and comparable laws include your consent (where required), performance of our employment or service contract with you, legitimate business interests in operating the Portal securely, and compliance with legal obligations.

4. Use of Google User Data

The Portal uses Google APIs so that authorized staff can perform their work. We request only the scopes strictly necessary for each feature:

Google scope / API Why we use it
openid, profile, email Sign staff in via Google SSO and match their account to an ESA user record.
Google Ads API Retrieve campaign, ad group, keyword, and performance data for the marketing team to review in the Portal.
Google Cloud / BigQuery Read analytics data from our managed BigQuery warehouse to populate dashboards. Accessed via a service account, not via end-user scopes.

Our use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. In particular:

  • We only use Google user data to provide and improve the user-facing features described in this Policy.
  • We do not transfer Google user data except to provide or improve these features, to comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets with notice to users.
  • We do not use Google user data for serving advertisements, including retargeting, personalized, or interest-based advertising.
  • We do not allow humans to read Google user data unless we have your affirmative consent for specific messages, it is necessary for security (such as investigating abuse), to comply with applicable law, or the data has been aggregated and anonymized for internal operations.

You can review and revoke the Portal's access to your Google account at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

5. Cookies and Similar Technologies

The Portal uses a small number of strictly necessary cookies and browser storage items:

  • Session cookie — a signed, HTTP-only cookie that keeps you signed in during a session.
  • CSRF token — used to protect forms from cross-site request forgery attacks.
  • Preference storage — local browser storage for UI preferences such as the selected site filter.

We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies on the Portal.

6. Disclosure of Personal Data

We do not sell personal data. We disclose personal data only in these circumstances:

  • Within the Extra Space Asia group, to staff who need the information to carry out their role.
  • To service providers acting on our behalf under written agreements that require confidentiality and data protection, including:
    • Microsoft Azure — cloud hosting and infrastructure.
    • Microsoft and Google — identity and authentication.
    • Google Cloud — BigQuery analytics.
    • StorageMaker Data (SMD) — property management system.
    • SugarCRM — customer relationship management.
    • EmbedSocial — customer reviews aggregation.
  • To regulators, courts, or law enforcement where required by applicable law or valid legal process.
  • In a corporate transaction such as a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.
  • To protect rights and safety — where we reasonably believe disclosure is necessary to prevent harm, fraud, or abuse of the Portal.

7. International Transfers

ESA operates in multiple jurisdictions in Asia. Personal data may be transferred to, stored in, or accessed from countries other than the one where it was collected, including Singapore (primary hosting region) and other territories where our staff, contractors, or service providers operate. Where required, we put in place contractual and technical safeguards so that transferred personal data continues to receive a standard of protection comparable to that under the PDPA.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described above and to meet legal, regulatory, accounting, or reporting requirements. General retention periods:

  • Active staff accounts — for the duration of employment or engagement, plus a reasonable period afterwards for handover and audit.
  • Authentication and audit logs — up to 24 months, unless a longer period is required by law or for ongoing investigations.
  • Operational business records — as required by Singapore accounting and tax law (generally 5 years) and comparable laws in other operating jurisdictions.
  • Diagnostic and error logs — typically 90 days.

When personal data is no longer required, we delete or irreversibly anonymize it.

9. Security

We protect the Portal using layered technical and organizational measures, including:

  • TLS/HTTPS encryption of all traffic in transit.
  • Encryption of sensitive credentials at rest using Fernet with a key derived via PBKDF2.
  • Role-based access control and least-privilege permissions.
  • Multi-factor authentication through our identity providers.
  • Rate limiting, CSRF protection, and input validation to defend against common web attacks.
  • Centralized audit logging of sensitive operations.
  • Periodic vulnerability scanning and internal security reviews.

No system can be guaranteed fully secure. If we become aware of a personal data breach affecting your information, we will notify affected individuals and the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC) of Singapore, and any other regulator as required by law.

10. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Withdraw consent, where processing is based on consent, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to retention obligations.
  • Lodge a complaint with the Singapore Personal Data Protection Commission at www.pdpc.gov.sg or with another competent supervisory authority.

To exercise these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer using the details in Section 12. We may need to verify your identity before acting on your request and will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law.

11. Children

The Portal is an internal workplace tool and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18 through the Portal.
